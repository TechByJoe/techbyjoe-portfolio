Metal Chair

This project involved conceptualizing and modelling a metal chair that could withstand up to 300 lbs. of simulated pressure while maintaining less than an inch of displacement. The challenge was that the model could not contain any supporting material below the seat of the chair. Additionally, the model had to be as thin and light as possible. In designing my chair, I chose aluminum for its lightweight properties and made use of archs and circular cut-outs to minimize the costs while maintaining structural integrity.
